The cursus honorum in one climb

The one win: name the rungs of the Republican cursus honorum in order, and say what each one constrained — the scaffold every later Roman political story hangs on.

Teach

The cursus honorum was a legally fixed career ladder, not a custom: quaestor → (aedile) → praetor → consul, with minimum ages and mandatory gaps between offices (Beard, SPQR, ch. 4–5). The censorship sat above as a later capstone.

The point isn't the list — it's the fixedness. Because you couldn't skip rungs, every ambition had to route through the sequence, at prescribed ages. That routing is precisely where late-Republic pressure built: men who couldn't advance fast enough by the rules (Pompey, Caesar) had reason to bend or break them, and the breaking is the story of the Republic's end.
