# SPQR: A History of Ancient Rome — Mary Beard

## What this source is

A single-volume modern history of Rome from its origins to the early 3rd century AD (Caracalla's 212 AD universal citizenship grant), written for a general but serious audience by one of the most prominent contemporary classicists. Beard is a Cambridge professor; the book is scholarly in its underpinnings but accessible in its prose.

## Why it matters here

This is the primary orienting modern secondary for Phases 1 through 4 of the study. Beard provides the institutional and analytical framing that the ancient sources don't always make explicit, and she is appropriately skeptical about the legendary material in early Roman history. Phase 1 uses Chapters 1–4.

Limit: Beard mostly stops in 212 AD, which means Phases 5 and 6 need different secondaries.

## Approach notes

- **Pace:** Read chapter by chapter, taking notes on institutional/structural points (not on chronology — that comes from Livy and Polybius)
- **Depth:** First read is for orientation; expect to return to specific chapters when working on related Units
- **Pairing:** Beard pairs well with the ancient sources — read Beard first on a period to get the framing, then read the ancient source with her interpretive caveats in mind
- **What to watch for:** Beard is skeptical of romanticization. She is good at puncturing comfortable narratives. If you find yourself disagreeing with her, mark it — the disagreement is the learning.
