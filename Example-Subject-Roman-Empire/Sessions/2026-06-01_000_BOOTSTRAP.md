## Assistant Observations

- The cartridge demonstrates the four-Unit-type shape (concept + thinker + period + event) deliberately, in contrast to a canonical-thinker shape (cybernetics, philosophy) or a skill-based shape (cuisine, music). A user wanting to set up *their own* historical subject can clone this structure.
- Phase 1 coverage stops at 146 BC (destruction of Carthage). This is a defensible boundary — it marks the "year of two annihilations" after which Rome had no peer rival, and the structural pressures that produce the Late Republican breakdown begin in earnest. A user studying this cartridge should expect Phase 2 (Late Republic to Augustus) to be the heaviest in terms of named figures and events.
- The seed Units include `## Open Questions` sections that are not rhetorical — they point at real historiographical debates the user will encounter (Carandini's wall, the historicity of Lucretia, defensive vs. aggressive imperialism, Polybius's Scipio-family bias). These are good entry points for Socratic dialogue once study begins.
