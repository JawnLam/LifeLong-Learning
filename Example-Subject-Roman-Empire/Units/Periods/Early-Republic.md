# Early Republic (509 – 264 BC)

## Character

The roughly two-and-a-half centuries between the end of the monarchy and the start of the First Punic War. Two parallel stories run through this period: (1) the **Conflict of the Orders** — the gradual political incorporation of plebeians into the magistracies and law-making process — and (2) the **conquest of Italy** — Rome's expansion from a single city to the master of the Italian peninsula. By 264 BC, both projects are largely complete, and Rome is poised to confront Carthage in the Mediterranean.

## Key Institutions in Force

- **Two annual consuls** (originally called *praetors*) replace the king's executive authority — collegiality and annual rotation as defenses against renewed monarchy
- **Senate** — increasingly influential advisory body of former magistrates
- **Comitia centuriata** — the centuriate assembly, organized by wealth/military classes (a Servian institution that becomes politically dominant)
- **Comitia tributa** — the tribal assembly, organized by geographic tribes
- **Tribunes of the plebs** (created 494 BC) — magistrates with sacrosanct status and veto power, exclusively plebeian
- **Twelve Tables** (451–450 BC) — first written Roman law, breaks patrician monopoly on legal knowledge
- **Dictator** — emergency office with concentrated imperium, time-limited (originally 6 months max)

## Major Events Within

- [[Expulsion-Of-Tarquins]] (509 BC) — establishment of the Republic
- First Secession of the Plebs (494 BC) — creation of the tribunate
- Twelve Tables (451–450 BC) — codification of law
- [[Sack-Of-Rome-By-Gauls]] (~390 BC) — formative trauma; Brennus, "vae victis"
- Licinian-Sextian Laws (367 BC) — one consulship opened to plebeians
- Samnite Wars (343–290 BC) — the wars by which Rome wins central and southern Italy
- Pyrrhic War (280–275 BC) — Pyrrhus of Epirus invades on behalf of Tarentum; Rome wins despite "Pyrrhic victories"
- Lex Hortensia (287 BC) — plebeian assembly's resolutions binding on all Romans; conventional end of the Conflict of the Orders

## Inflection Out

Two near-simultaneous developments by 264 BC:
1. **Italy unified under Rome.** The system of alliances and treaties (the *civitas sine suffragio*, Latin League dissolution, the colonial system) gives Rome control of the peninsula.
2. **Rome enters Mediterranean politics.** The First Punic War begins in 264 BC, dragging Rome into conflict with Carthage and Mediterranean naval power. This conventionally ends the Early Republic and begins the Middle Republic.

## Concepts Operative in This Period

- [[Patrician-Plebeian-Distinction]] (the Conflict of the Orders runs through this period)
- [[Cursus-Honorum]] (takes shape as offices open to plebeians)
- [[Mos-Maiorum]] (consolidates into the unwritten constitution that holds the system together)
- [[Imperium]] (granted to consuls and praetors; tested in the Italian wars)
